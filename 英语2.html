Situation 1:
You are a Chinese freshman at the very start of your first semester. At an English corner on campus, you meet an international PhD student from Egypt. After introducing yourselves, you mention that you've just finished military training. Seeing his curiosity, you explain this tradition in detail. During the conversation, practice asking for and giving clarifications by saying things like "Am I explaining this clearly?" Add helpful details if needed to keep the conversation friendly and smooth.

你是一名刚刚开始第一学期的大一中国学生。在校园英语角，你遇到了一位来自埃及的国际博士生。自我介绍后，你提到自己刚结束军训。看到他很好奇，你详细解释了这一传统。在对话中，练习通过说"我讲得清楚吗？"这类话来请求和给予说明。如有需要，可补充一些有帮助的细节，以保持对话友好顺畅。

A (Chinese freshman): Hi there! I’m Li Hua, a freshman majoring in Computer Science. Nice to meet you at the English corner!
你好呀！我是李华，计算机科学专业的大一新生。很高兴在英语角认识你！

B (Egyptian PhD student): Hello Li Hua! I’m Amr from Egypt. I’m pursuing a PhD in Environmental Science here. You just mentioned military training—what exactly is that? I’ve never heard of it before.
你好，李华！我是来自埃及的阿姆尔，正在这里攻读环境科学博士学位。你刚才提到了军训——那到底是什么呀？我以前从来没听过。

A: It’s a special tradition for Chinese freshmen right after entering university. We usually spend 2-3 weeks living on campus and receiving training from military instructors. Am I explaining this clearly?
这是中国新生入学后的一项特殊传统。我们通常会花2-3周时间住在校园里，接受教官的训练。我解释得清楚吗？

B: Mostly! But why do universities require this? Is it only about physical exercise?
大致清楚啦！但为什么大学会要求这个呢？只是为了锻炼身体吗？

A: Great question! Physical fitness is part of it, but it’s more about building discipline and teamwork. For example, we have to wake up at 6 a.m. every day, line up quickly, and do group exercises together. We also learn basic military skills like folding quilts into neat squares and saluting.
问得好！锻炼身体是一部分原因，但更重要的是培养纪律性和团队合作精神。比如，我们每天早上6点就得起床，快速列队，一起做集体训练。我们还会学习基本的军事技能，比如把被子叠成整齐的“豆腐块”和敬礼。

B: Folding quilts into squares? That sounds interesting and challenging! Did you find it tiring?
把被子叠成方块？听起来既有趣又有挑战性！你觉得累吗？

A: Honestly, it was tiring at first—especially the long hours of standing in the sun. But it got better as we got used to it. I even made some close friends during the training because we helped each other through the tough parts.
说实话，一开始挺累的——尤其是长时间站在太阳下。但慢慢习惯后就好多了。我还在训练期间交到了几个好朋友，因为我们互相帮助度过了那些艰难的时刻。

B: That makes sense! It seems like a unique way to help freshmen adapt to university life and bond with each other.
原来是这样！这似乎是帮助新生适应大学生活、增进彼此感情的一种独特方式。

A: Exactly! It also teaches us to respect rules and cherish the sense of community. Would you like to see some photos I took during the training? I have them on my phone.
没错！它还教会我们遵守规则，珍惜集体归属感。你想看看我军训时拍的照片吗？我手机里有存。

B: Sure! I’d love to get a closer look at this special tradition.
当然想！我很想更直观地了解这个特殊的传统。

Situation 2:
You are a Chinese freshman at the international dorm's charging area. As you are about to plug in your e-bike, an American student approaches and asked to use the last charging spot for his scooter. Discuss your urgent needs politely. During the conversation, practice expressing agreement (e.g. "You're right, that could work") and polite disagreement (e.g. "I see, but my battery is completely dead"). Find a workable compromise on timing through friendly and clear communication.

你是一名大一中国学生，在国际宿舍的充电区。你正准备给自己的电动车充电时，一位美国学生走过来，想用最后一个充电位给他的滑板车充电。礼貌地讨论你的紧急需求。在对话中，练习表达同意（例如："你说得对，这办法可行"）和礼貌的异议（例如："我明白，但我的车完全没电了"）。通过友好清晰的沟通，就充电时间找到一个可行的折中方案。

Situation 2

A (Chinese freshman): Excuse me, I was just about to plug in my e-bike. It’s the last charging spot, right?
打扰一下，我正准备给我的电动车充电呢。这是最后一个充电位了，对吗？

B (American student): Hi there! Yes, I noticed it too. I was hoping to charge my scooter—my battery is almost dead, and I have a group project meeting in 40 minutes across campus.
你好呀！是的，我也看到了。我想给我的踏板车充电——电池快没电了，而且40分钟后我要去校园另一头开小组项目会议。

A: I totally get it—group meetings are really important, and being late isn’t ideal. I see, but my battery is completely dead too. I need to go to the downtown bookstore to pick up a required textbook before it closes in an hour. If I don’t charge now, I won’t make it on time.
我完全理解——小组会议确实很重要，迟到不太好。我明白你的情况，但我的电池也彻底没电了。我得在一小时内赶到市中心的书店买一本必备教材，不然书店就关门了。如果现在不充电，我肯定赶不上了。

B: Hmm, that’s a dilemma. I guess neither of us can wait too long. What if we split the time? I only need 20 minutes to get enough charge for my meeting. Then you can use the rest of the time for your trip downtown.
嗯，这确实有点难办。看来我们俩都等不起。要不我们分一下时间？我只需要充20分钟，电量就够去开会了。然后剩下的时间就给你充电，足够你去市中心了。

A: You’re right, that could work! 20 minutes is manageable for me to wait. I can check my course schedule on my phone while I wait. Will 20 minutes really be enough for your scooter to get to the meeting?
你说得对，这个办法可行！等20分钟我还是能接受的。我可以趁等的时间在手机上看看课程表。20分钟对你的踏板车来说，真的够开到开会的地方吗？

B: Absolutely! My scooter has a low-power mode that’s perfect for short trips. I just need enough to get there and back later. I really appreciate you being flexible about this.
当然够啦！我的踏板车有低电量模式，特别适合短途出行。我只需要充够往返的电量就行。非常感谢你这么通融。

A: No problem at all! I’m glad we could figure something out together. I’ll keep an eye on the time—when 20 minutes are up, I’ll let you know right away.
不客气！很高兴我们能一起想出解决办法。我会留意时间的——20分钟一到，我马上告诉你。

B: Thanks a lot! I’ll hurry up and plug in now. I promise I won’t take a minute longer than planned.
太感谢了！我现在就赶紧插上充电。我保证绝不超时一分钟。

Situation 3:
You are a Chinese freshman in your first semester, chatting with a new classmate from the UK in a campus common area. The international student feels overwhelmed by university life and asks for your advice. Practice asking for and giving advice. Inquire about their specific challenges, then offer practical tips from your own experience, such as managing time or joining student clubs. Politely check whether your suggestions are helpful.

你是一名第一学期的大一中国学生，在校园公共区域与一位来自英国的新同学聊天。这位国际学生对大学生活感到不知所措，并向你寻求建议。练习如何请求和给予建议。询问他们遇到的具体挑战，然后根据自己的经验提供实用建议，例如时间管理或加入学生社团。礼貌地确认你的建议是否有帮助。

Situation 3

A (Chinese freshman): Hey Tom! You look a bit stressed out. Is everything okay with university life so far?
嘿，汤姆！你看起来有点压力山大。到目前为止，大学生活还顺利吗？

B (UK classmate): Hi Lin Mei! Honestly, I’m feeling a bit overwhelmed. There are so many assignments, club recruitments, and new people to meet—I don’t know how to balance it all.
嗨，林梅！说实话，我有点招架不住了。有太多作业要写、社团要招新，还要认识好多新朋友——我不知道该怎么平衡这些事情。

A: I totally get that feeling! I felt the same way when I first started. Can you tell me more about your specific challenges? Is it the coursework, time management, or something else?
我完全能理解这种感受！我刚开学的时候也这样。你能跟我说说具体遇到什么困难了吗？是课程作业、时间管理，还是其他方面的问题？

B: It’s mostly time management. I end up spending too much time on one assignment and then rushing through the others. Also, I want to join a sports club, but I’m worried I won’t have enough time for studying.
主要是时间管理的问题。我常常在一个作业上花太多时间，然后其他作业就只能匆匆赶完。而且我想加入一个体育社团，但又担心没有足够的时间学习。

A: That’s totally normal! Here’s a tip from my experience: try making a weekly schedule on your phone or a notebook. Write down all your classes, assignment deadlines, and club activities. This way, you can see exactly how much time you have for each task. Would that be helpful for you?
这太正常了！分享一个我的经验技巧：试试在手机或笔记本上做每周计划表。把所有的课程、作业截止日期和社团活动都写下来。这样你就能清楚地知道每项任务有多少时间可以用。这个方法对你有用吗？

B: That sounds practical! I’ve never thought of planning that carefully before. What about balancing clubs and studies?
听起来很实用！我以前从来没想过要这么细致地规划。那怎么平衡社团和学习呢？

A: You can start by choosing a club that meets once or twice a week—like the football club, which only has training sessions on Tuesday and Thursday evenings. Also, most clubs are flexible if you need to skip a session for exams or assignments. Another thing: don’t be afraid to say “no” to extra activities if you’re too busy. Your studies are important, but it’s also okay to take time for yourself.
你可以先选一个每周只活动一两次的社团——比如足球社，他们只在周二和周四晚上训练。而且大多数社团都很灵活，如果你因为考试或作业需要缺席一次活动也没关系。另外一点：如果太忙的话，别害怕拒绝额外的活动。学习固然重要，但也可以给自己留一些放松的时间。

B: That makes a lot of sense! I think the weekly schedule will be a game-changer for me. Thank you so much for the advice.
太有道理了！我觉得这个每周计划表会给我带来很大改变。非常感谢你的建议。

A: You’re welcome! Let me know if you need help setting up the schedule, or if you have any other questions later. I’m sure you’ll get the hang of it soon!
不客气！如果你需要帮忙制定计划表，或者之后有其他问题，随时告诉我。我相信你很快就能适应的！
